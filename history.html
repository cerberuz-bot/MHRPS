HISTORY

The Most Holy Rosary Parochial School was founded by Most Rev. Emilio Z. Marquez, DD, then Bishop of Gumaca. In 1983, experimental classes were organized at the the Catholic Center pioneered by four retired teachers in the person of Mrs. Isidra L. Olivo, Mrs. Rosario A. Florido, Mrs. Ester Orolino and Miss Felisa Sevilla.
By 1986, Nursery and Kindergarten classes for children aged 3-5 years old were offered during the first year of the school's existence. The school was officially recognized by the government when the Securities and Exchange Commission certified the Articles of Incorporation and by-laws of Most Holy Rosary Parochial School, Inc. on July 7, 1987.
In 1990, the Department of Education, Culture and Sports granted recognition to the school's pre-elementary program. Two years later, the school obtained recognition for its elementary program. High school classes opened in the school year 1996-1997 and was granted recognition on May 5, 2000.
From the Catholic Center, the school was transferred to its present side behind the church building. Two and three storey buildings, as well as various improvements were subsequently added to school facilities.
Over the years the administration of the school had been handled by the Missionary Catechists of St. Therese (MCST) sisters serving as principals, financial officers, and teachers.
